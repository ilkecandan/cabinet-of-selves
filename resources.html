Welcome to Your Inner World: Exploring Internal Family Systems (IFS)

If you’ve ever found yourself thinking, “Part of me feels one way, but another part of me feels something completely different,” you’re already touching on what Internal Family Systems (IFS) is all about.

IFS is a beautiful and compassionate therapeutic approach that invites you to get to know the many parts that make up your internal world — like unique voices within you, each with their own stories, feelings, and needs.

Developed by Dr. Richard Schwartz, IFS works from the understanding that we are all made up of different inner parts, and that these parts form a kind of internal family system, each playing their role to help you navigate life.

Why Do We Have Parts?

Life shapes us. Experiences, both big and small, leave marks on how we see ourselves and the world. To survive difficult moments, we naturally develop parts that step in to protect us.

What Is Self?

In IFS, Self is the core of who you are — the calm, confident, compassionate essence underneath all the noise of your parts.

How to Begin Your Own IFS Journey

1. Notice Your Parts

Start paying attention to your inner dialogue. When you feel anxious, sad, or conflicted, ask:

“Is there a part of me feeling this way?”

2. Get Curious, Not Critical

Be curious about your parts instead of judging them. Ask:

“What are you trying to protect me from?”

3. Befriend Your Parts

Parts need to feel seen and heard. Let them know you care.

4. Identify Protectors and Exiles

  • Protectors: Parts that defend you (like the inner critic).
  • Exiles: Parts that carry pain from past experiences.

5. Invite Self to Lead

When you relate to your parts with curiosity and compassion, your Self becomes the loving leader they need.
