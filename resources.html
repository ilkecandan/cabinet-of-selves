Welcome to Your Inner World: Exploring Internal Family Systems (IFS)

If you’ve ever found yourself thinking, “Part of me feels one way, but another part of me feels something completely different,” you’re already touching on what Internal Family Systems (IFS) is all about.

IFS is a beautiful and compassionate therapeutic approach that invites you to get to know the many parts that make up your internal world — like unique voices within you, each with their own stories, feelings, and needs.

Developed by Dr. Richard Schwartz, IFS works from the understanding that we are all made up of different inner parts, and that these parts form a kind of internal family system, each playing their role to help you navigate life. Some parts are protectors, trying to shield you from pain or rejection. Others carry wounds from past experiences, holding deep sadness, fear, or shame. And then, at the very core, there is your Self — the calm, compassionate center of who you are, capable of healing and guiding all of these parts with love and understanding.

Why Do We Have Parts?

Life shapes us. Experiences, both big and small, leave marks on how we see ourselves and the world. To survive difficult moments, we naturally develop parts that step in to protect us. Some become inner critics, trying to keep us safe by pushing us to be perfect. Others become caretakers, making sure everyone else’s needs come before our own. Some parts hide away, frozen in time, holding painful memories that were too overwhelming to face.

These parts aren’t the problem — they’re actually trying their best to help. But over time, they can become stuck in patterns that no longer serve us, leaving us feeling conflicted, overwhelmed, or disconnected from our true Self.

What Is Self?

In IFS, Self is the core of who you are — the calm, confident, compassionate essence underneath all the noise of your parts. Self is the natural leader within you, the part that holds wisdom, clarity, and kindness. The goal of IFS is to help your Self become the loving guide your parts need, so they can relax and find new, healthier roles.

How to Begin Your Own IFS Journey

1. Notice Your Parts

Start paying attention to your inner dialogue. When you feel anxious, sad, critical, or conflicted, pause and ask yourself:

“Is there a part of me feeling this way?”

Just noticing and acknowledging your parts is a wonderful beginning.

2. Get Curious, Not Critical

When you notice a part, be curious rather than judgmental. You might ask:

“I see you there, anxious part. What are you worried about?”

Curiosity helps parts feel safe enough to open up.

3. Befriend Your Parts

Parts need to feel seen and heard. Let them know:

“I’m here with you. I want to understand what you’ve been carrying.”

4. Identify Protectors and Exiles

  • Protectors: They keep you safe through criticism, perfectionism, people-pleasing, or avoidance.
  • Exiles: These tender, often younger parts carry past wounds — loneliness, rejection, shame, fear.

5. Invite Self to Lead

As you build relationships with your parts, gently invite your Self to step forward. Self brings calm, clarity, compassion, and courage.

Check in with yourself:

  • Am I feeling curious?
  • Can I feel compassion for this part?
  • Can I stay calm and present?

If so, Self is leading. If not, be gentle — parts need time to trust.

This is tender work. If it feels overwhelming, pause and tell your parts:

“I’m here. We can go at your pace.”

Journaling, drawing, or naming your parts can deepen your connection over time.
